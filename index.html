A day in San Francisco

The perfect Day

San Francisco

7:30 am

- Golden Gate Bridge -

The first Stop might be he best one hehe, The Golden Gate Bridge ! At that time, our advance would be to just take a uber, it might cost you around $5, but trust me it's worth it.

Why this one first, right? Well first, because the parking lot is free until 9am. Secondly, the bridge can get very crowed over the course of the day.


" Are you my Uber Driver ? " -> "Etes-vous mon uber ?"

" Can you please take a photo of us ? " -> " Pouvez-vous nous prendre en photo ? "


9:00 am

9:00 am

- Breakfast -

After walking the Bridge, it's Breakfast Time ! If you love pancakes as much as you love Venom, the perfect address is Outerlands. The best way to get there from the Bridge would be to get a uber and it's going to cost you around $4.

Just like Venom, their pancakes are out of this World :) I can literally wait for a whole hour in brunch line to eat one.


" Can we have the menu please ? " -> " Pouvons-nous avoir le menu ? "

" Can we have the addition please ? " -> " Pouvons-nous avoir l'addition ? "

10:30 am

10:30 am

- Golden Gate Park -

Next stop, but not least, Golden Gate Park ! To go there you literally just have to walk 5 minutes, ask google :) Once you're out of Outerlands, take the 45th Avenue in direction of Lincoln Way.

Always wanted to go to Central Park in New York City ? Well what if I tell you that the Golden Gate Park is just as beautiful, bigger and full of surprises. Indeed, you can literally find Bison in the Park, no need to pay tickets to Yellowstone to see some. As well, as a lake, 2 museums, a conservatory and much more.


" Where can I find the conservatory ? " -> " Où puis-je trouver le conservatoire ? "

" Do they have water here ? " -> " Je peux trouver de l'eau ? "


11:30 am

11:30 am

- Haight Street -

After the exploration, it's time for some fantasy... Welcome to Haight Street. From the Golden Gate Park, hop on the #7 bus to get there.

Also know as the "Hippie Street of San Francisco", because of its trippy murals, Tibetan clothes stores, apothecary and much more. The best to discover all its surprises, is to start at the top of Haight at Stanyan and walk down. But be aware of the homeless people there, also called "wannabe hippies", hanging out in groups playing musics smocking weed and beg people for money to buy more weed.


" How much cost that dress ? " -> " Combien coùte cette robe ? "

" Where can I find some weed ? " -> " Où puis-je trouver du thé ? "


1:00 pm

1:00 pm

- Hayes valley -

After buying your best outfit, hop on a #7 bus and take it to Geary Street. Get off and walk the 2 blocks to Hayes Valley

Take a break and chill in the cutest part of San Francisco. Indeed, it's always quiet and peaceful, it's like this place has a shield against all the movements around it. If you're hungry, feel free to stop by for lunch at Souvla, the best Greek spot in the city.


" Where can I take the bus 7 ? " -> " Où puis-je prendre le bus 7 ? "

" Where can I find the Solvia restaurant ? " -> " Où puis-je trouver le restaurant le Solvia ? "


2:00 pm

2:00 pm

- Union Square -

For the next stop you'll have to, once gain, take the bus #7, or if u want to discover San Francisco's underground, walk to the Van Ness train station and take any of the MUNI trains to the Powell Street stop. Welcome to Union Square !

No need to wait, as soon as you get on the surface or get out of the bus, turn your head left, on Market and Powell. You will see one of the satisfying think you ever saw, the rotation of a Cable-Car. Our exploration will start by the plaza, it's to San francisco what Time Square is to New York City. Indeed, we can find art shows, a huge tree during christmas and even ice skating. Then, keep walking to the North East corner to Stockton Street. Walk up Stockton 2 block and take a right on Bush for one block until you reach the Dragon’s Gate. You can’t miss it’s the entrance to Chinatown!!


" Is this MUNI's going to Powell Station ? " -> " Ce train va-t-il a la station Powell ? "

" Where can I find the Plaza ? " -> " Où puis-je trouver le Plaza ? "


3:30 pm

3:30 pm

- North Beach -

After taking your best pictures at Union Square and Chinatown, hop on the bus #8 until Bay Street & Midway Stree. There you go, after being in China, you are now in Italy :)

North Beach is very unique. Since it’s near the Bay, there used to be the center of the Barbary Coast era (dance halls, concert saloons, bars, jazz clubs, variety shows, and brothels, to name a few.) Some buildings have underground tunnels that were used to sneak bootlegged products out to ships (or vice versa.) If you can, this guided walking tour of North Beach is really fun.


" What stop should I take to go to North Beach ? " -> " Où devrais-je m'arrêté pour aller à North Beach ? "


4:30 pm

4:30 pm

- Lombard Street -

For our next stop u will just have to walk, take Columbus Ave to Lombard Street. And if you can't find it just follow the people from North Beach, they will

you to it :)

it’s time to walk up a really big hill to Lombard Street, aka the most crooked Street in America! This is a must see in San Francisco as you can’t find anything like it in the world. Lombard Street is a short street that winds back and forth. From the bottom of the hill, you can look up and see the “waves” of the road.


" How can I reach Lombard Street ? " -> " Comment puis-je atteindre Lombard Street ? "


4:00 pm

4:00 pm

-Fisherman’s Wharf-

How to get to the next stop, right ? Just walk down the hill from Lombard. I'm sure you were already down before reading this, huh ? ;)

Everyone spending a day in San Francisco wants to go see Fisherman’s Wharf. I can’t help but make my disclaimer about this neighborhood: it’s touristy AF. Locals don’t ever come here as it’s kind of one big tourist trap. That being said, on the flip side, it is fun to kill a couple of hours here on a nice day. You can swing by Ghirardelli square to pick up some free chocolate samples (yum!) and then walk down Jefferson Street along the Bay. This area has amazing views of the Bay, Golden Gate Bridge and Alcatraz.


" How much cost a ticket for Alcatraz ? " -> " Combien coute un ticket pour Alcatraz ? "


6:30 pm

6:30 pm

-Dinner Time-

Must be hungry huh ? At this time i advice you to take a uber, to one of the best Mac and cheese place, Mac Daddy in Potrero Hill. The ride will cost you around $7.

Tucked away in Potrero Hill is a joint called Mac Daddy that only serves mac & cheese. You read that right – they specialize in mac & cheese here! *Mic drop* It’s my favorite little hidden gem in the city and most tourists don’t visit because it’s not downtown. You’ll need to take an Uber over here but it’s well worth it for the food but also because this neighborhood is really pretty. I recommend the Goat mac & cheese with short ribs added in it omg I’m drooling just thinking about it.


" What kind of Mac Cheese do you have ? " -> " Quels sont les type de Mac and Cheese avez-vous ? "


8:00 pm

8:00 pm

-Twin Peaks-

For our last stop and maybe the best of them, I'm gonna show you the best view you could ever have of San Francisco. Hop in a uber to the Twin Peaks.

I love coming to these tall hills for sunsets. It makes the city look so small because when you’re running around it, it feels big. It’s actually just 7×7 miles and from the top of a hill, you can actually take it all in. You’re allowed to drink in the parks, so feel free to bring a bottle of wine or a 6 pack of beer to the top of the hills, sit down and enjoy the show.


No tips for this once, juste enjoy :)
